The newspaper clipping is the key here. L’Ouest-Éclair was a real regional French newspaper based in Brittany, and the details in the clipping — a lifeboat disaster, seven rescuers drowned, Finistère coast — point to a well-documented historical tragedy: the Penmarc’h lifeboat disaster of May 23, 1925.

On that date, two rescue boats — one from Kérity and one from Saint-Pierre — capsized during a rescue operation in brutal conditions. Both Kérity and Saint-Pierre are villages within the commune of Penmarc’h, located in the Finistère department of Brittany.

Once you have the location, the rest is straightforward. A quick lookup of Penmarc’h’s postal code gives you 29760.

He was the youngest aboard, and for his bravery during the disaster, he was awarded a silver medal.
